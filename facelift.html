What is a Facelift?

A facelift, or rhytidectomy, is a surgical procedure designed to reduce visible signs of aging in the face and neck. Over time, gravity, sun exposure, and natural aging cause the skin to lose elasticity, resulting in sagging skin, wrinkles, and loss of definition. A facelift addresses these concerns by tightening the underlying facial muscles and removing excess skin, resulting in a more youthful, refreshed appearance.

Our surgeons use advanced techniques to create natural-looking results that enhance your features without appearing overdone or artificial.

Procedure Overview

Surgical Technique

A traditional facelift involves carefully planned incisions, typically hidden in the hairline and around the ears. Dr. Sterling lifts and tightens the underlying facial muscles and removes excess skin, creating a more youthful contour.

Duration

The procedure typically takes 2 to 4 hours, depending on the extent of the surgery. Most patients stay overnight for observation and go home the next day.

Anesthesia

Facelift surgery is performed under general anesthesia or twilight anesthesia, ensuring your comfort and safety throughout the procedure.

Customization

Every facelift is customized to address your specific concerns, whether that's sagging jowls, deep nasolabial folds, neck laxity, or overall facial rejuvenation.

Ideal Candidates for Facelift

Facelift is suitable for individuals who:

  • Are experiencing visible signs of aging in the face and neck
  • Have sagging skin or loss of facial definition
  • Want to address deep wrinkles and folds
  • Have jowls or neck laxity
  • Are in good overall health
  • Have realistic expectations about surgical outcomes
  • Are typically 40 years or older, though age is not the only factor
  • Don't smoke or are willing to quit before and after surgery

During your consultation, Dr. Sterling will assess your candidacy and discuss whether facelift is the right choice for you.

Recovery and Aftercare

First Week

You'll experience swelling, bruising, and some discomfort, which is normal. Pain is typically managed with prescribed medication. You'll need to keep your head elevated and avoid strenuous activities.

Weeks 2-3

Most patients can return to light activities and may feel ready to go out in public. Swelling continues to decrease, though some residual puffiness may remain.

Weeks 4-6

You can gradually resume normal activities, including exercise. Most of the visible swelling has subsided, and you'll begin to see your final results.

Months 2-6

Continued improvement as residual swelling resolves. Final results typically appear after 3-6 months as all internal healing is complete.

Post-Operative Instructions

  • Keep your head elevated at all times, even while sleeping
  • Apply ice packs as directed to minimize swelling
  • Wear compression garments as recommended
  • Avoid strenuous activities and heavy lifting for 3-4 weeks
  • Don't smoke or expose yourself to secondhand smoke
  • Avoid direct sun exposure for at least 2 weeks
  • Attend all follow-up appointments
  • Take prescribed medications as directed

Frequently Asked Questions

Will facelift scars be visible?
Facelift incisions are carefully placed in natural creases and the hairline to minimize visibility. Scars fade significantly over time and are typically not noticeable, especially when your hair is down.
How long do facelift results last?
Facelift results typically last 7-10 years or longer. While aging continues naturally, most patients are very satisfied with their results for many years.
Can I combine facelift with other procedures?
Yes, many patients combine facelift with eyelid surgery, brow lift, or other procedures for comprehensive facial rejuvenation. This can be done during the same surgery or staged over time.
When will I see final results?
You'll see significant improvement within 3-4 weeks, but final results typically appear after 3-6 months as all swelling resolves and the tissues settle into their new position.
What are the risks of facelift?
Like any surgery, facelift carries some risks, including infection, bleeding, and nerve injury. However, serious complications are rare when performed by a board-certified surgeon in an accredited facility.
