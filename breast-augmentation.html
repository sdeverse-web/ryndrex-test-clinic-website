What is Breast Augmentation?

Breast augmentation, also known as augmentation mammaplasty, is a surgical procedure designed to enhance the size and shape of the breasts. Whether you're looking to increase volume, improve symmetry, or restore fullness lost due to pregnancy or weight loss, breast augmentation can help you achieve your aesthetic goals.

Dr. Mitchell specializes in creating natural-looking, beautifully proportioned results that complement your body and enhance your confidence.

Procedure Overview

Implant Options

We offer both saline and silicone gel implants in various sizes and profiles. During your consultation, we'll discuss which option is best for your body type and aesthetic goals.

Surgical Approach

Implants can be placed through incisions under the breast, around the areola, or in the armpit. Dr. Mitchell will recommend the best approach based on your anatomy and preferences.

Implant Placement

Implants can be placed above or below the pectoral muscle. Submuscular placement often provides a more natural appearance and better long-term results.

Duration

The procedure typically takes 1 to 2 hours. Most patients go home the same day with a responsible adult to drive them.

Ideal Candidates for Breast Augmentation

Breast augmentation is suitable for individuals who:

  • Want to increase breast size and volume
  • Desire improved breast shape and symmetry
  • Want to restore fullness lost due to pregnancy or weight loss
  • Are unhappy with the natural size of their breasts
  • Are in good overall health
  • Have realistic expectations about surgical outcomes
  • Are at least 18 years old (21 for silicone implants)
  • Are not currently pregnant or breastfeeding

During your consultation, Dr. Mitchell will assess your candidacy and discuss whether breast augmentation is the right choice for you.

Benefits of Breast Augmentation

Enhanced Silhouette

Breast augmentation creates a fuller, more proportionate silhouette that enhances your overall appearance and body confidence.

Improved Symmetry

If your breasts are naturally asymmetrical, augmentation can create better balance and proportion.

Restored Fullness

Pregnancy and weight loss can affect breast volume. Augmentation can restore the fullness you had before or always wanted.

Boosted Confidence

Many patients report a significant increase in self-confidence and body satisfaction after breast augmentation.

Natural-Looking Results

Dr. Mitchell focuses on creating results that look natural and beautifully proportioned to your body.

Long-Lasting Enhancement

Breast implants are durable and designed to last many years, providing long-term aesthetic enhancement.

Recovery and Aftercare

First Week

You'll experience soreness, swelling, and tightness in the chest area. Pain is managed with prescribed medication. You'll wear a surgical bra for support and healing.

Weeks 2-4

Most patients can return to light activities and desk work. Avoid strenuous exercise and heavy lifting. Swelling continues to decrease.

Weeks 4-6

You can gradually resume normal activities, including light exercise. Most of the discomfort has resolved.

Months 2-3

You can return to all activities, including strenuous exercise. Final results become apparent as swelling fully resolves.

Post-Operative Instructions

  • Wear the surgical bra as directed for proper support
  • Take prescribed medications as directed
  • Avoid strenuous activities and heavy lifting for 4-6 weeks
  • Don't sleep on your stomach for at least 2 weeks
  • Avoid swimming and hot tubs until incisions are fully healed
  • Perform gentle massage as instructed to promote healing
  • Attend all follow-up appointments
  • Report any unusual symptoms to your surgeon immediately

Frequently Asked Questions

Will breast augmentation affect breastfeeding?
Breast augmentation typically does not affect your ability to breastfeed, especially if implants are placed below the muscle. However, it's important to discuss this with Dr. Mitchell during your consultation.
How long do breast implants last?
Modern breast implants are designed to last many years. While they don't have a specific expiration date, some patients choose to replace them after 10-15 years or if complications occur.
What size implants should I choose?
Implant size is a personal choice based on your body frame, aesthetic goals, and preferences. Dr. Mitchell will help you select the right size during your consultation using 3D imaging technology.
Will breast augmentation scars be visible?
Incisions are carefully placed to minimize visibility. Scars fade significantly over time and are typically hidden by swimsuits, bras, or natural skin folds.
What are the risks of breast augmentation?
Like any surgery, breast augmentation carries some risks, including infection, bleeding, and capsular contracture. However, serious complications are rare when performed by a board-certified surgeon.
